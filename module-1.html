Claude Chat

The one you already know. Let's make sure you're using it well — and getting out of your own way when Cowork or Code would serve you better.

A quick recap of the interface

Claude Chat lives at claude.ai. It's a browser-based conversation window. You type a message, Claude responds, and you can go back and forth as many times as you want in a single conversation thread.

You can attach files (PDFs, images, documents), paste in URLs, and share conversation history with others. If you're using the team plan, your conversations are private to you unless you share them.

Pro move Claude remembers everything within a single conversation. Start a new chat only when you're starting a genuinely new task — staying in one thread means Claude retains context and you don't have to re-explain yourself.

When chat is the right tool

✓ Chat is great for...

  • Quick drafts you can review and send right away
  • Brainstorming and thinking out loud
  • Explaining something you pasted in
  • Answering a client question when you're mobile
  • Rewriting a paragraph until it's right
  • Translating, summarizing, or reformatting text
  • One-off tasks that don't need to be repeated

✗ Chat gets clunky when...

  • You need Claude to read files from your hard drive
  • The task has 5+ steps and you want to walk away
  • You're doing the same thing every week (use Cowork)
  • You want to deploy an actual thing to a URL (use Code)
  • The conversation hits its context limit and Claude "forgets"
  • You need version history or GitHub integration

OO-specific examples

Here are three real situations where Chat is exactly the right call. Each example shows the actual prompt you'd use — not a vague template, a real one.

Example: Drafting a proposal section
"I'm writing the SEO strategy section of a proposal for [Client Name], a regional orthodontics group with locations in New Orleans and Baton Rouge. Their main goal is more first-patient calls from local search. Write a 3-paragraph section explaining our approach — local SEO, Google Business Profile optimization, and conversion-focused landing pages. Tone should be confident but not salesy. We're trying to win their trust, not close a deal."

Why chat works: This is a one-shot draft task. You paste it in, review the output, tweak it, and paste it into your doc. Done.

Example: Writing a social caption
"Write 3 variations of a LinkedIn caption for Online Optimism announcing that we were named a Best Place to Work for the fifth year in a row. Keep it celebratory but not braggy — we want to credit the team, not ourselves. Include 1-2 relevant emojis per caption. No hashtags. Sentence case only."

Why chat works: Quick creative task, three options to choose from, no files involved. This is Chat's home turf.

Example: Answering a client email
"Here's an email from a client asking why their Google Ads CPC jumped 40% this month. [Paste the email] They're clearly frustrated. Draft a response that: acknowledges the jump, gives two or three factual explanations (competition, seasonality, Quality Score), and sets up a brief call to walk through our optimization plan. Keep it calm, professional, and not defensive. We value this client relationship."

Why chat works: You paste the email in, Claude drafts a response with the right tone, you edit and send. No file access needed.

The 5 things that make a Claude Chat prompt actually work

  • Role + goal: Tell Claude who you are and what you're trying to accomplish. "I'm an AE at a digital marketing agency writing a proposal for a restaurant client."
  • Audience: Who is this for? A skeptical CMO reads differently than a small business owner who's never worked with an agency.
  • Tone constraints: "Confident but not salesy," "warm but professional," "casual — this is for Instagram, not LinkedIn."
  • Format: "3 bullet points," "2 short paragraphs," "a numbered list," "50 words max." Claude writes to whatever container you give it.
  • What NOT to do: If there's a common version you hate ("don't start with 'Absolutely!'"), say so explicitly. Claude will listen.

Claude Chat is the one you already know — it's claude.ai, you type, it responds. Super quick, works great on your phone, no setup required. The key is knowing when to stick with chat and when to upgrade to one of the other tools. Chat is perfect for anything you can knock out in a single conversation — a draft, a rewrite, a client email response, a social caption. If it fits in a chat window and you're working from copy you can paste in, chat's your move. Where people get frustrated is using chat for things it's not built for: reading files off your desktop, running a multi-step workflow, or building something that actually has to go live somewhere.
